Scoring

A brief view of how the system reads pressure.

The scoring model gives structure to what the diagnostic sees. It helps qualify operating pressure, support needs, and likely implementation fit without revealing the full methodology.

What it looks for

The scoring process looks at operational friction, clarity of ownership, consistency of follow-through, and the amount of pressure the business is currently carrying.

It is designed to turn vague symptoms into a clearer starting point.

What it does not do

It does not replace judgement. It does not publish the full weighting model. And it does not reduce the business to a single number without context.

Lower pressure

Usually indicates a more stable operating environment where diagnostic-led clarity may be enough to define the next move.

Moderate pressure

Usually points to structure gaps that need reinforcement before performance becomes more reliable.

Higher pressure

Usually signals stronger implementation needs, heavier operating strain, or a greater need for support and control.

The exact scoring model stays inside the system. Visitors get enough context to understand the approach, without giving away the full framework.
